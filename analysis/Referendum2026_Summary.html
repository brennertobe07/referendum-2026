Key findings
  • A base-mobilization electorate, not a new one. 76% of voters had voted in 3-or-4 of the last 4 November generals; first-time voters were just 2.1%.
  • Republicans turned out harder, Democrats' larger base carried it. Rep turnout 53% vs Dem 45%, but Dem-leaning registrants outnumber Republicans ~3.7M to 2.7M and were the majority of voters (53% vs 47%). Geography was sharply partisan — strongest Yes in cities/NoVa, strongest No in southwest Virginia.
  • Turnout climbed steeply with age (29% at 18–24 to 72% at 65–74); women turned out 2.0 pp higher than men (52.8% vs 50.8%).
  • Method skewed old-early, young-day: 55% election day, 35% in-person early, 10% mail.
